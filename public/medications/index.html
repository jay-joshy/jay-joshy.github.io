Bilastine

Brand Names: Blexten

Cost: ~$1 CAD per 20mg tab (ON)

Mechanism of Action: H1 antagonist. Marketed as limited CNS distribution.

Half-life: ~15 hours

Routes: oral tab, liquid (uncommon)

Doses:

  • CSU, AR: 20mg daily to QID (adult dosing)
  • CSU, AR: 10mg daily to QID (pediatric dosing)

Age Group: >=12 years of age; has been used off-label in younger children

Pregnancy: Avoid (not enough data)

Contraindications: Hypersensitivity, QT prolongation or history of torsades

Common Side Effects: QT prolongation; drowsiness (4%); headache (4%). For reference, drowisness/headache had a 2% incidence rate in placebo arm

Severe Side Effects: Torsades (very rare)

Monograph Links:

  • https://pdf.hres.ca/dpd_pm/00062419.PDF

Fexofenadine

Brand Names: Allegra, Telfast

Cost: ~$1.20 CAD per 180mg tab (ON)

Mechanism of Action: H1 antagonist. Non-sedating antihistamine.

Half-life: ~14 hours

Routes: oral tab, liquid, ODT

Doses:

  • AR, CSU: 180mg once daily (adult dosing)
  • AR, CSU: 30mg twice daily (pediatric dosing (ages 2-11))

Age Group: >=2 years of age

Pregnancy: Considered safe (Category B)

Contraindications: Hypersensitivity to fexofenadine

Common Side Effects: Headache (~1%), dizziness (<1%)

Severe Side Effects: Rare hypersensitivity reactions

Monograph Links:

  • https://example.com/fexofenadine-monograph

Loratadine

Brand Names: Claritin, Alavert

Cost: ~$0.50 CAD per 10mg tab (ON)

Mechanism of Action: H1 antagonist with minimal CNS penetration.

Half-life: ~8 hours (active metabolite ~27 hours)

Routes: oral tab, liquid, disintegrating tab

Doses:

  • AR, CSU: 10mg once daily (adult dosing)
  • AR, CSU: 5mg once daily (pediatric dosing (ages 2-5))

Age Group: >=2 years of age

Pregnancy: Considered safe (Category B)

Contraindications: Hypersensitivity to loratadine or desloratadine

Common Side Effects: Headache, dry mouth, fatigue (~2-3%)

Severe Side Effects: Rare anaphylaxis, hepatic dysfunction

Monograph Links:

  • https://example.com/loratadine-monograph
